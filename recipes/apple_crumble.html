Apple Crumble

Description

Embrace the essence of fall with the warm and comforting aroma of the apple crumble, a timeless dessert that captures the season's bounty in each spoonful. This classic treat takes the humble apple to new heights as its slices meld with a luscious blend of cinnamon and sugar, nestled beneath a golden quilt of buttery crumble. The result is a symphony of textures and flavors, where the tender sweetness of baked apples harmonizes with the crunchy perfection of the topping.

Whether served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream, the apple crumble is a celebration of simplicity and the rich, cozy flavors that define autumn. Get ready to savor the taste of the season with this delightful dessert that effortlessly transforms any gathering into a heartwarming occasion.

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • Apples: 800g
  • Sugar: 50g
  • Cinnamon: 5mL
  • Lemon juuice: 15mL

For the crumble topping:

  • Oats: 100g
  • Flour: 150g
  • Sugar: 100g
  • Butter (cold sliced): 100g
  • Cinnamon: 2.5mL
  • Salt:0.3g

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven:
    Preheat the oven to 200C.
  2. Prepare the apples: Peel, core, and slice the apples.
  3. Add sugar and flavour:
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, ground cinnamon, and lemon juice. Toss the mixture until the apples are evenly coated.
  4. Make the crumble topping:
    In another bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, rolled oats, cold diced butter, sugar, and a pinch of salt. Use your fingers or a pastry cutter to mix the ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  5. Assemble the crumble:
    Transfer the apple mixture to a baking dish and set aside. Spread it out evenly. Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the prepared apples.
  6. Bake:
    Bake in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes or until the top is golden brown, and the apples are tender.
  7. Cool and serve:
    Allow the apple crumble to cool slightly before serving. This dessert pairs wonderfully with vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.
